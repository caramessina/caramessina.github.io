About Me

Cara Marta Messina [she/her]
  • Doctoral candidate at Northeastern University's English Department
  • Writing Studies + New Media
  • Fan studies, rhetorical genre studies, critical digital pedagogy, cultural studies, and anti-racist and anti-misogyny advocate.

My overall research and pedagogy concerns the representation and celebration of counternormative narratives. I am currently working on my dissertation in which I plan to explore why fanfiction (and other fan media) is meaningful to its creators and readers. One of my goals is to focus on each writers' perspective and use that perspective to then conduct quantitative analyses using methods like topic modeling, word vectors, and more. For some of the preliminary practices of these methods, check out my topic model visualizations for over 7,000 The Legend of Korra fanfictions I collected from Archive of our Own.

This year, I am the Coordinator for the NULab for Texts, Maps, and Networks. As the Coordinator, I help co-lead the Digital Teaching Initiative, which is in its pilot stages working with Humanities and Social Science faculty who are interested in including digital tools and methods in their classroom.

As for some of the organizational work I do at Northeastern, I am currently the President of the English Graduate Student Association President. This year, we are organizing the annual EGSA conference; the theme we chose is "Centering the Margins" to highlight the work of people who are historically, poltiically, and socially marginalized. I am also helping to co-lead the Feminist Coding Collective (formerly the Digital Feminist Commons), which prioritizes creating an inclusive feminist space for people to learn and practice coding.

In the past, I have worked in numerous writing centers, including at Columbia University's School of Social Work and SUNY Empire. My heart is always with writing center work, so I still invest publishing and conference time and labor into the discipline. My first year at Northeastern, I held the position of Director of Research at the Writing Center at Northeastern where I and members of NuRig – the writing center research inquiry group – collected data about how clients and consultants articulate their understanding of the work done in writing centers.

I have taught First Year Writing and Advanced Writing in the Disciplines at St. John's University and Northeastern University. In my FYW courses, which you can read more about here, I integrate fan practices to empower and engage students so they think critically about how narratives may reinforce or subvert racism, sexism, homophobia, transphobia, and ableism.

I have also worked closely on two projects at Northeastern:

  • I was a Research Associate for Design for Diversity, an IMLS-grant funded national forum project, at Northeastern’s Digital Scholarship Group. I helped organize the Opening Forum event in October 2017 (read my reflection of the event here). I also helped to build and design the early stages of the Design for Diversity Teaching and Learning Toolkit, which centers around equitable and just practices in information systems.
  • I also worked on the Pedagogy and Infrastructure Teams for the. Women Writers Vector Toolkit. I wrote several assignments, helped build the website, wrote and narrated scripts to help explain the Word Vector Interface, and more.
