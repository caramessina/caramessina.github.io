About Me

Cara Marta Messina [she/her]
  • Doctoral candidate at Northeastern University's English Department
  • Writing and Rhetoric + Digital Humanities + New Media
  • Fan studies, rhetorical genre studies, critical digital pedagogy, writing analytics, coding rhetorics, and anti-racist and anti-misogyny advocate.

Research: My overall research and pedagogy concerns the representation and celebration of critical and counternormative narratives. My dissertation will create a Critical Fan Toolkit that fans can use to become more critical in their practices and teachers can use to incorporate critical fan pedagogies.

Current Position: I am the Assistant Director of the Digital Teaching Integration, part of Northeastern University's College of Social Sciences and Humanities initiative to better integrate digital tools, methods, and research in classrooms across the disciplines.

Pedagogy: I received the 2019 Kairos Graduate Student Teaching Award. I have taught First Year Writing and Advanced Writing in the Disciplines at St. John's University and Northeastern University. In my FYW courses, which you can read more about here, I integrate fan practices to empower and engage students so they think critically about how narratives may reinforce or challenge racism, sexism, homophobia, transphobia, and ableism.

Service: I am the current founder and chair of the Northeastern English Department's Graduate Student Welfare Group, where we create newsletter to celebrate graduate students and provide resources; lead or organize workshops around various topics such as microagressions and Title IX/campus resources around sexual harassment; and collaboratively write statements to support particular groups of students or organizations. I also contributed to the WPA-L Working Group, particularly the participation guidelines
