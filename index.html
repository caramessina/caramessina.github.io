Cara Marta Messina

Cara Marta Messina [she/her] is a fourth year doctoral candidate focusing on Writing and Rhetoric in Northeastern University’s English Department. Her focus is Writing Studies and Digital Humanities, particularly fan genres and publics. Her research interests include–but are not limited to–fan studies, critical digital pedagogy, digital and coding rhetorics, #transformDH, and representations of counternormative narratives.

She is currently working on her digital dissertation titled "The Critical Fan Toolkit: Fanfiction Genres, Ideologies, and Pedagogies." This toolkit will incorporate interactive data, interviews with fans, teaching materials, and other resources for fans and instructors alike. Critical fan practices are the ways in which fans resist harmful and exclusive cultural ideologies in their uptakes; specifically, critical fandom practices are the thoughtful integrations the politics of power, gender, sexuality, disability, and race in their fans’ participations in fan genres in order to combat homophobia, transphobia, sexism, racism, and ableism (Paul Booth, 2012; andré carrington, 2013; Alexis Lothian, 2016).

Outside of her work, Cara watches a bit too much Sailor Moon and brings her pup, Efi, everywhere. Her hobbies revolve around reading, gaming, falling into coding holes, boxing, and imagining that she will someday make her own cosplays. She loves her friends dearly and believes professional and academic settings should celebrate friendships and kinships.
